About Me

Hi, I’m a Marie Skłodowska-Curie PhD Researcher at the University of Ferrara in collaboration with the University of Bologna, within the FutureData4EU programme. My research lies at the intersection of astrophysics and data science, with a focus on multimessenger transient phenomena. I am particularly interested in double white dwarf merger remnants and kilonova modelling, using statistical methods,and numerical simulations. These systems are promising progenitors of faint, rapidly evolving optical transients.

A key aspect of my current work involves time-dependent radiative transfer simulations of double white dwarf merger ejecta using POSSIS (POlarization Spectral Synthesis In Supernovae), a three-dimensional Monte Carlo radiative transfer code. In the era of wide-field surveys such as the Legacy Survey of Space and Time (LSST) at the Vera C. Rubin Observatory, such modelling is quite essential for identifying and interpreting these elusive transients.

Beyond research, I enjoy doing music and travelling, which help me stay creatively inspired and connected to the world beyond science.
