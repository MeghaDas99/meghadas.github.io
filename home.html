About Me

Hi, I am a Marie Skłodowska-Curie PhD Researcher within the FutureData4EU programme. My research focuses on multimessenger transient astrophysics, particularly double white dwarf merger remnants and kilonova modelling, with expertise in data reduction and statistical modelling. While some mergers may lead to Type Ia supernovae, a large fraction are expected to form long-lived remnants. These systems are promising progenitors of faint, rapidly evolving optical transients, yet their observational signatures remain poorly constrained.

A major uncertainty lies in the efficiency with which radioactive decay energy in the ejecta is thermalized and converted into observable radiation, directly shaping peak luminosities, colour evolution, and inferred ejecta properties.

My work involves time-dependent radiative transfer simulations of double white dwarf merger ejecta using POSSIS (POlarization Spectral Synthesis In Supernovae), a three-dimensional Monte Carlo radiative transfer code.

In the era of wide-field surveys such as the Legacy Survey of Space and Time (LSST) at the Vera C. Rubin Observatory, such modelling is essential for identifying and interpreting these elusive transients.

Beyond research, I enjoy doing music and travelling, which help me stay creatively inspired and connected to the world beyond science.
